Complete a chore

  1. Open the client at / (or the Chores tab on Android). Today's assigned chores appear in the main list, grouped by type.
  2. Tap or click the chore you have finished.
  3. If the chore requires proof — a camera icon is shown — attach a photo from your camera or gallery before submitting. Chores marked as requiring proof will not accept a completion without one.
  4. Tap Complete. The chore moves out of the active list, your points balance increases, and your streak is extended if this is your first completion of the day.
  5. If the chore is part of a chain (for example, Hang clothes follows Wash clothes), the follow-up task is automatically scheduled for the assigned member once you submit.

On Android, the speed dial FAB gives you quick access to the most common completion shortcut so you can finish a task without navigating through the list.

Handle overdue chores

Chores whose due time has passed without a completion appear in the Overdue section above the regular chore list on both web and Android, highlighted with a prominent alert style so they stand out.

  1. Tap the overdue chore to open it — the completion flow is the same as a regular chore.
  2. Completing an overdue chore still awards full points unless the admin has configured an overdue penalty in household settings.
  3. If you cannot complete the chore yourself, use Request takeover (see below) to hand it to another member.

Request or grant a takeover

A takeover lets one household member cover another's assigned chore.

  1. Open the chore you want covered (it can be your own chore or one you are volunteering to cover).
  2. Tap Request takeover. A notification is sent to the relevant member.
  3. The receiving member sees the request on their device. They can accept, which reassigns the chore to them, or decline, which returns it to the original assignee.
  4. Once accepted, the chore appears in the new member's active list and points for completion are credited to whoever finishes it.

Redeem a reward (child / member)

  1. Open the Rewards tab (web or Android). Your current points balance appears at the top of the page.
  2. Browse the enabled rewards. Each card shows the reward name, description, point cost, and an eligibility badge if the reward is restricted to a specific member type.
  3. Rewards you cannot yet afford are shown with a dimmed Redeem button. A cooldown notice appears on any reward you have redeemed recently and must wait before redeeming again.
  4. Tap Redeem on the reward you want. A confirmation dialog shows the point cost and your remaining balance after the redemption.
  5. Confirm the redemption.
    • If the household uses automatic approval, points are deducted immediately and you receive a confirmation notification.
    • If the household requires approval, a pending request is created. Your points are held until an admin or parent approves or rejects it. The request appears in your History tab with a Pending status.

Check redemption history

  1. Open the Rewards tab and switch to the History tab (web) or expand the My Redemptions section (Android).
  2. Each entry shows the reward name, the points deducted, the request date, and the current status: Pending, Approved, or Rejected.
  3. Rejected requests show the admin's note if one was provided.

Approve or reject a reward redemption (admin / parent)

When a household member redeems a reward and approval is required, a badge appears on the Rewards nav item showing the number of pending requests.

  1. Open the admin UI at /admin and navigate to Rewards, then switch to the Approvals tab. On Android, the pending queue appears in the Rewards tab for admin and parent users.
  2. Each pending row shows the member's name, the reward they requested, the point cost, and the request time.
  3. Tap Approve to deduct the points from the member's balance and send them an approval notification.
  4. Tap Reject to decline the request without deducting points. An optional note field lets you explain the rejection — the member sees this note in their history.

Enable starter rewards

TaskBandit seeds nine starter rewards into every new household, all disabled by default: extra screen time (30 min and 1 hour), a treat of their choice, pick tonight's dinner, pick the movie, choose the family activity, stay up 30 minutes later, skip a chore, and pocket money bonus.

  1. Open the admin UI and navigate to Rewards.
  2. The catalogue is grouped by category. Each row has a toggle on the right.
  3. Flip the toggle next to any starter reward to enable it. It becomes immediately visible in the member-facing shop.
  4. To adjust the point cost, click the reward row to open its editor and change the Point cost field, then save.

Create a custom reward

  1. In the admin Rewards page, click + Add custom reward.
  2. Fill in the reward editor on the right:
    • Title — what the member sees in the shop (e.g. Choose Saturday's activity).
    • Description — optional extra context shown below the title.
    • Category — groups the reward in the catalogue (screen time, treat, activity, privilege, allowance, or custom).
    • Point cost — how many points the member spends.
    • Max redemptions per child — optional lifetime cap per member. Leave blank for unlimited.
    • Cooldown (days) — optional minimum gap between redemptions of this reward. Leave blank for no cooldown.
    • Eligibility — All members, Children only, or Adults only. Children-only rewards are hidden from parent and admin members in the shop; adult-only rewards are hidden from child members.
  3. Click Save. The reward is created but disabled. Flip the toggle in the catalogue list to make it visible in the shop.

Turn off reward approval (auto-approve mode)

If you trust members to redeem without a parent review — for example in an adult household — you can disable the approval step entirely.

  1. Open the admin UI and navigate to Settings.
  2. Find the Require reward approval toggle and turn it off.
  3. From that point, all new redemptions are approved instantly and points are deducted at the moment of submission. Existing pending requests in the queue are not affected automatically — approve or reject them manually.

Import starter chore templates

Starter templates are available during first-household setup and can also be imported later from the admin Templates page.

  1. During the household setup wizard, a list of starter template groups is shown with checkboxes. Select the routines that apply to your household (laundry, kitchen, cleaning, bedding, bins, plants, and others).
  2. Click Import selected. The templates are added to your household's chore catalogue as normal editable templates.
  3. If you skip the import during setup, you can still access it later from the admin Templates page using the Import starters button.
  4. All starter templates are translated into English, German, and Hungarian. The language used in the member-facing chore cards follows each member's language preference.

Set up a chore chain

A chore chain automatically schedules a follow-up task when a preceding chore is completed — useful for routines like laundry (wash → hang → fold) or kitchen cleanup (cook → wash up → put away).

  1. Open the admin Templates page and find or create the chore that should trigger the follow-up (e.g. Wash clothes).
  2. In the template editor, find the Follow-up field. Select the template that should be scheduled next (e.g. Hang clothes).
  3. Choose the assignment for the follow-up: same member, round-robin, or a specific member.
  4. Save. Now whenever a household member completes Wash clothes, Hang clothes is automatically added to the schedule for the assigned member.

Invite a household member

  1. Open the admin UI and navigate to Members.
  2. Click Invite member and enter their email address and the role you want to assign them (child, parent, or admin).
  3. An invitation email is sent. The recipient clicks the link, creates a password, and joins the household automatically.
  4. If email is not configured, you can generate a one-time invite link from the same dialog and share it manually.

Connect the Android app to your server

  1. Install the APK from the latest GitHub release — find it on the Releases page or directly at github.com/kriziw/TaskBandit/releases. Enable Install from unknown sources in your Android settings if prompted.
  2. Open the app. On the sign-in screen, tap the server URL field and enter your public API base URL (e.g. https://api.taskbandit.example.com).
  3. Enter your household email and password, then tap Sign in. The app connects, downloads the household state, and lands on the Chores tab.

Use the speed dial FAB

The floating action button (FAB) in the bottom-right corner of the Android app expands into a shortcut menu on tap.

  1. Tap the FAB. It fans out into labelled shortcut buttons for common actions.
  2. Tap the relevant shortcut — for example, to mark the topmost due chore complete without scrolling to it.
  3. Tap anywhere outside the expanded FAB, or tap the FAB again, to collapse it.

Update the Android app in-app

When a newer APK is available, a floating update bubble appears in the corner of the app.

  1. Tap the update bubble. A prompt confirms the version and asks whether to download now.
  2. Tap Download & install. The APK is downloaded in the background.
  3. When the download completes, a system install prompt appears. Accept it to update the app.
  4. If you dismiss the bubble, it reappears the next time you open the app until the update is installed.

Use the app offline

The Android app queues actions when there is no network connection and flushes them automatically when connectivity is restored.

  • Chore completions and reward redemption requests submitted while offline are saved locally and sent to the server the next time the app has a connection.
  • Points and streaks are updated once the queued actions are successfully synced.
  • If a queued action conflicts with a server-side change (e.g. the chore was already completed by another member), the server resolves the conflict and the app updates accordingly.
